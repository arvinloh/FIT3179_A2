New South Wales has the highest total number of births (99,309), followed by Victoria (81,789) and Queensland (63,383).

Peak Live Births
  • The majority of live births occur at gestational ages 39 weeks (98,495 live births, 31.4%) and 38 weeks (73,523 live births, 23.5%).
  • These two gestational ages together account for over half of all live births.
    • Fetal Deaths
  • The highest number of fetal deaths occurs at very early gestational ages, particularly at 20 weeks (336 fetal deaths, 14.9%) and 21 weeks (341 fetal deaths, 15.1%).
  • Fetal deaths significantly decrease as gestational age increases, with very few fetal deaths occurring at 39 and 40 weeks (44 fetal deaths each, 1.9%).
    • Gestational Age 39 Weeks
  • Gestational age 39 weeks has the highest percentage of live births (31.4%) and a very low percentage of fetal deaths (1.9%).
  • This indicates that gestational age 39 weeks is the safest gestational age for live births.
    • Normal BMI Range (18.5-24.9)
  • For mothers with a normal BMI range, the majority of live births occur at gestational ages 39 weeks and 38 weeks.
  • The majority of mothers fall within the normal BMI range (18.5–24.9), with a total of 140,985 mothers in Australia.
    • Birth Weight
  • Birthweight is an important indicator of an infant’s health.
  • In 2021, over 9 in 10 babies (91.99%) were born with a normal birthweight (birthweight between 2,500 and 4,499 grams); also referred to as 'healthy birthweight'.
  • The majority of live births have a birth weight between 3,000 and 3,499 grams (37.42%).
  • Very few live births have a birth weight below 2,500 grams (6.81%) or above 4,500 grams (1.2%).
